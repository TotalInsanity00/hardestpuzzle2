The Map

Every map lies a little, but the compass trusts the oracle. No saint will guide you here; the choir hums toward noon. Doors reflect doors; a mirror only answers with echo. Walk until the road repeats; patterns bloom due north. On quiet nights, ink thinks it’s thunder, but it’s only ink. Rumors wear masks; names change, the face keeps its nest. Drift past the siren’s red herring; trust the final ember.
